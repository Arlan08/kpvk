Internal regulations

These Rules have been developed in accordance with the Law of the Republic of Kazakhstan "On Education", the Charter of the College, which determine the internal regulations of the Kostanay Polytechnic Higher College (hereinafter referred to as the College), the basic rights and obligations of students, as well as regulate other issues related to the activities of students at the College.

The internal regulations are mandatory for students to follow.


1. General provisions

1.1 These Rules are the main local normative act, which determines the internal daily routine of students at the Kostanay Polytechnic Higher College

1.2 Students (persons enrolled in College by order of the director) are required to master knowledge, complete all types of tasks provided for in curricula and educational programs within the prescribed time frame.

1.3 Academic discipline is provided by the College administration by creating the necessary organizational and economic conditions for normal high-performance work, conscious fulfillment of academic duties by methods of persuasion, education, as well as encouragement for conscientious work and training. Disciplinary measures are applied to violators of discipline.

1.4 Issues related to the application of Internal Regulations are resolved by the College administration within the limits of the rights granted to it, and in cases provided for by the current legislation of the Republic of Kazakhstan and Internal Regulations - jointly or in coordination with the students' trade union Committee and the College Youth Affairs Committee.

1.5 The rules of the internal regulations of College students, as well as all changes and additions to them, are adopted by the engineering and pedagogical Council on the recommendation of the administration and come into force from the moment the director signs the order.

1.6 The text of the Internal Regulations of students is posted on bulletin boards at the College.


2. Basic rights of students

College students have the right to:

2.1 To receive education in accordance with the state mandatory standards of education of the Republic of Kazakhstan;

2.2 To receive additional paid educational services under the contract;

2.3 The right to respect for one's human dignity, freedom of conscience, information, and the free expression of one's own opinions and beliefs;

2.4 To participate in the discussion and resolution of the most important issues of the college's activities, including through public student organizations and college management bodies;

2.5 For free use of the library, information funds, educational, medical and other departments at the college;

2.6 The opportunity to participate in all types of creative projects, scientific research, conferences, symposiums, meetings, competitions, festivals, presentation of their works for publication, including in college publications;

2.7 The opportunity to speak out about the quality and methods of teaching and make suggestions for their improvement;

2.8 To create student self-government bodies and student public organizations;

2.9 Choose elective courses offered by College teachers;

2.10 To elect and be elected to the governing bodies of the college;

2.11 Freely attend events organized at the College as part of educational work or student recreation.


3. The main responsibilities of students

College students are required to:

3.1 Perform the duties stipulated in the Law of the Republic of Kazakhstan "On Education".

3.2 To comply with the College Charter, the Rules of Internal Regulations at the college, the Code of Academic Integrity of Students, to comply with the decisions of the Engineering and Pedagogical Council, orders and orders of the director, deputy directors regulating educational, creative, scientific processes, their organization and conduct; to honorably bear the name of a College student; to comply with the rules of residence in a student dormitory.

3.3 Students of all forms of College education are required to master knowledge, complete all types of tasks on time, pass all exams and tests in strict accordance with curricula and study programs on time (examination session).

3.4 To raise the cultural and professional level.

3.5 Comply with the requirements for occupational safety, safety, industrial sanitation, occupational hygiene and fire protection provided for by the relevant rules and instructions approved by the Director of the College.

3.6 Immediately report to the College administration on the circumstances that impede or impede normal operation (accident, other emergencies), and take the necessary measures to eliminate them.

3.7 To keep their educational equipment (technical means of training) in good condition, to maintain cleanliness at the educational place, to observe the established procedure for storing material valuables and documents.

3.8 To protect the property of the College, preventing its damage, to use equipment and machinery effectively, to take care of instruments, concert costumes and other items given to students for use, to use energy, fuel, water and other resources economically and rationally.

3.9 Respect human dignity and the opinion of students, teachers, college staff, and be tolerant of the opinions of others.

3.10 While in the academic building, dormitory and other buildings of the College, behave with dignity, refrain from actions that prevent other students from fulfilling their duties, follow generally accepted moral and ethical standards, and take care of the business reputation of the College.

3.11 Be disciplined, keep clean and tidy in all academic and other premises of the College, regularly take part in clean-up and other activities for cleaning educational and auxiliary premises, dormitories and academic buildings of the College and the surrounding area.

3.12 Students should be neat, follow a business style of clothing (light monochrome shirt (blouse), dark monochrome trousers (skirt), red tie, jacket), behave decently in College, on the street, in public places and at home.

3.13 It is prohibited in the premises of the College:

- It is strictly forbidden to bring piercing and cutting objects into the territory and premises of the college;

- loud conversations, noise, use of obscene language, walking through the corridors during classes;

- acts of physical violence or threats by some against others, ill-treatment and insults to each other, aggressive or hooligan behavior, extortion, rude behavior, shouting, spitting, etc.), theft of property of others, deliberate damage to personal property of other people and the College;

- smoking in the academic building, dormitory, in the wrong places;

- the use of alcoholic beverages, alcohol, the use of toxic and narcotic drugs;

- gambling

3.14 The student must respect the student's academic integrity and not allow:

- cheating, using cheat sheets and other illegal ways to obtain information in order to help yourself or someone else while doing academic work, during intermediate or final certification;

- plagiarism, i.e. theft or impersonation of someone's ideas or conclusions, presentation of the relevant work as a result of one's own thoughts and ideas;

- any other forms of misconduct, which may include: forgery of documents or data, lying, copying, changing or any other use of stored information (including computer information) without permission. As well as other similar acts that can be reasonably attributed to such violations.


4. Public bodies of student self-government

4.1 KDM in its work is guided by the legislation of the Republic of Kazakhstan, these Rules and other regulatory legal acts.

4.2 The KDM consists of persons elected at the general meeting of students and group elders.

4.3 Youth Affairs Committee::

- coordinates the activities of the group heads;

- attracts students on a voluntary basis to perform socially useful work at the College (cleaning of classrooms) and in the surrounding area;

- helps the College administration in the organization of control over the safety of material assets;

- represents the interests of students;

- assists in the organization of cultural and mass work.


5. Study schedule, study time and its use

5.1. The academic year begins on September 1, consists of two semesters (each semester includes 1 milestone certification) and ends according to the curriculum no later than July 1. The Engineering and Pedagogical Council of the College has the right to decide on changing the schedule of the educational process.

5.2. The educational process at the College is carried out for 6 days a week (Monday to Saturday) in accordance with the curricula and class schedule. Registration on weekends and holidays is allowed in exceptional cases in accordance with the legislation of the Republic of Kazakhstan.

5.3. The College establishes the following main types of educational activities:

1) theoretical lesson;

2) practical lesson;

3) laboratory lesson;

4) control work;

5) consultation;

6) independent work;

7) educational practice;

8) industrial practice (according to the profile of the specialty);

9) industrial practice (pre-graduate);

10) diploma design;

11) course work (project);

12) final qualification work, etc.

Forms of control: exam, credit, independent work, control work, test, defense of final qualification work, state exam;

5.4. The schedule of classes is approved by the director and posted at the designated place no later than 3 days before the start of classes.

5.5. After the start of classes, all classrooms and adjacent premises must be provided with the silence and order necessary for the normal course of training sessions. It is unacceptable to interrupt training sessions, enter and leave the classroom during their conduct.

5.6. Students can enter and exit the classroom after the actual start of classes only with the permission of the teacher.

5.7. For practical, seminar, etc. types of classes in classrooms each course is divided into academic groups, if necessary â€“ into subgroups. The composition of academic groups and subgroups is determined by the order of the Deputy Director for Academic Affairs.

5.8. In each academic group, a prefect is appointed from among the most successful and disciplined students. The head of the group reports directly to the curator and carries out all his orders and instructions within the framework of the College Charter.

5.9. In each academic group, a log of student performance and attendance is kept in the prescribed form, which is stored in the academic part and is issued to the teacher daily before the start of classes to mark grades in it, as well as those present and absent from classes.

5.10. If the student fails to attend classes for valid reasons, no later than the next day, the student notifies the curator of the group about this and on the first day of attendance, submits data on the reason for the absence and documents of the established sample (certificates, summonses, letters, telegrams, etc.) containing information of an exculpatory nature.


6. Rewards for academic success

6.1 Various forms of moral and material encouragement are established for College students:

6.2 The forms of moral encouragement for students are:

- Declaration of gratitude;

- awarding with a letter of thanks;

- awarding with a diploma;

- posting information about a student's achievements on the college's website;

- posting information about student's achievements in the media;

- awarding a letter of thanks to the student's parents.

6.3 The forms of financial encouragement for students are

- awarding with a valuable gift (if funds are available);

- awarding with a cash prize (if funds are available);

- awarding students with sightseeing trips, theater tickets, etc. (if funds are available).

6.4 Gratitude: - for success in the development of educational programs, high results in public, educational, research, sports activities, industrial practice; assistance in the improvement of the college;

6.5 Diploma: - for success in mastering educational programs, high results in educational, professional and social activities at the college and city levels.

6.6 Certificate of merit: - for high indicators of high-quality academic performance of students throughout the entire period of study at the Kostanay Polytechnic Higher College, high results of participation in events of the regional and republican level.

6.7 Diploma: - for high results in creative, sports, aesthetic activities, social activities of the college, the group;

6.8 A letter of thanks to the student's parents for raising their son (daughter) for the student's high results in mastering educational programs, participation in city, regional, republican events, exemplary behavior, impeccable compliance with the requirements of the Charter of the Kostanay Polytechnic Higher College, Internal Regulations throughout the entire period of study;

6.9 An open letter to parents: - for high-quality results based on the results of the intermediate certification, active participation in all types of activities;

6.10 Entering the student's surname and first name on the "College Walk of Fame" for excellent achievements in the development of educational programs, experimental design and other social activities;

6.11 Financial incentives (if funds are available) can be combined with forms of moral encouragement, assigned for high results in the development of educational programs, effective participation of students in events at the city, regional, regional level, a group of students who took first place in the "Best Group of the Year" competition, and the best student in the "Best Student of the Year" competition;

6.12 Material and moral forms of encouragement for students can be individual and group in nature, depending on the content and type of activity.

6.13 The grounds for the promotion are:

- academic success;

- absence of absences from classes and disciplinary punishments;

- participation in conferences, Olympiads, competitions;

- active social activities aimed at creating a favorable image of the college (student council, Open days, military-patriotic circle, festivals, concerts, exhibitions, contests, Olympiads, sports events and competitions, etc.)

6.14 According to the presentation of the KDM, the study groups that have won prizes in college-wide events are awarded.

6.15 At the request of the heads of departments, students who have achieved high results in educational activities are awarded.

6.16 In case of team achievements, a reward is assigned to each member of the team.

6.17 Candidates for encouragement can be nominated by teachers, curators, heads of creative circles, sports sections, the head of the group, KDM, deputy director for Internal Affairs.

6.18 Simultaneous application of several incentive measures is allowed.

6.19 The incentives are announced by order of the director, brought to the attention of the entire team and stored in the student's personal file.


7. Responsibility for violation of academic discipline

7.1 For non-fulfillment of curricula, violations provided for by the Charter of the College, duties, Internal Regulations, Code of Honor, various disciplinary measures can be applied against students, up to expulsion from the educational institution:

- remark

- a reprimand with entry in a personal file

- registration on the intra-college account

- College dropout

7.2 Students may receive disciplinary action in the following cases:

- a single or systematic failure to fulfill obligations provided for by these Internal Regulations, the Code of Honor of Students;

- the use of the educational space for other purposes;

- destruction or damage to the educational premises;

- appearance in a state of alcoholic or narcotic intoxication;

- skipping classes for no good reason in the amount of 30 hours or more;

- storage and distribution of narcotic drugs;

- in other cases provided for by the legislation of the Republic of Kazakhstan.

7.3 The application of disciplinary penalties is formalized by the order of the director of the College.

7.4 For negligent treatment of documents (loss of a student ID card, record book, etc.), a disciplinary measure may be applied to students. In case of loss of the document, its duplicate can be issued only once during the entire period of the student's studies at the College.

7.5 When applying disciplinary measures, the severity of the offense committed, the circumstances under which it was committed, previous studies and the behavior of the student are taken into account.

7.6 It is not allowed to expel students during their illness (documented), vacations, academic leave or maternity leave.

7.7 The grounds for the expulsion of persons studying at the College, including in the case of their committing illegal actions, are determined by the Charter of the College and these Internal Regulations.

A student may be expelled from College:

- at his own request;

- in connection with the transfer to another educational organization;

- for health reasons (academic leave);

- for academic failure (according to regulatory requirements);

- for violation of the terms of the contract, if the training is carried out on the basis of full cost recovery;

- for violations of internal regulations;

- in other cases provided for by the legislation of the Republic of Kazakhstan.
