The Code of Academic Integrity of Students

Kostanay Polytechnic College (hereinafter referred to as the College), striving to ensure the high quality of educational programs, scientific research, as well as creative and applied projects, guarantees compliance with the principle of academic integrity. The high quality of educational programs, scientific research, creative and applied projects is achievable exclusively in the context of a culture of academic integrity shared by all members of the academic community of the College. Violations of academic standards harm the credibility and reputation of the College as an educational institution that adheres to high standards of teaching, research and creativity.


I. General provisions

1.1. The College's Code of Academic Integrity (hereinafter referred to as the Code) sets ethical standards applicable to the relations between the administration, faculty, staff and students in their academic activities.

1.2. This Code applies to all members of the academic community of the College â€“ administration, students, teachers, staff.


II. Principles of academic integrity

2.1. The main principles of academic honesty of students in the educational process, which develop their personal honesty and responsibility for their studies, are:

- Conscientiousness is the honest, thorough performance by students of evaluated and unappreciated works. The main components of conscientiousness are diligence and decency.

- the implementation of the protection of the rights of the author and his legal successors is the recognition of authorship and protection of works that are the object of copyright, through the correct transmission of someone else's speech, thoughts and indication of sources of information in the evaluated works;

- openness - transparency, mutual trust, open exchange of information and ideas between students, teachers and equivalent persons;

- respect for the rights and freedoms of students - the right of free expression of opinions and ideas by students;

- equality is the obligation of each student to comply with these Rules and equal responsibility for their violation.


III. General standards of academic ethics

3.1. The educational, scientific and creative activities of the members of the academic community of the College are based on the responsible implementation of the principle of academic freedom. This is contradicted, in particular, by the following:

- intolerant attitude towards different opinions;

- lack of information about the possibility of participation in academic self-government, or the establishment of unreasonable restrictions on the implementation of such an opportunity;

- unjustified limitation of the opportunity to respond to criticism or complaints.

3.2. The relations between the members of the academic community of the College are based on the principles of justice, mutual respect and solidarity. These principles are violated, in particular, in the following cases:

- discrimination based on age, race/color, ethnicity, gender, sexual orientation, religious or other views, special needs in making administrative and academic decisions at the College;

- the use of offensive language by members of the College's academic community in their communications;

- the impact of personal antipathy or interpersonal differences between students;

- public disclosure of confidential information about students and teachers.


IV. Ethical standards of student's educational activity

4.2 The assessment of a student's knowledge, skills and abilities must be fair, fair and based on the criteria set out in the course program. Therefore, the student must:

- strictly observe academic integrity in the performance of evaluated and unappreciated works;

- use a method of transmitting someone else's speech and thoughts with an indication of the author, the title of the work and the page;

- at the end of the evaluated works, indicate the list of references;

- use reliable and reliable sources of information;

- do not provide other students with the actual work being evaluated;

- independently perform all types of tasks;

- do not use ready-made answers to tests, exercises and assignments, ready-made written works (essays, term papers, theses);

- do not use cheat sheets, electronic means of information and communication in exams;

- do not share your ready-made answers and papers with other students and do not allow behavior that calls into question the honesty and integrity of the training and harms the final results of other students;


V. Ethical standards of scientific activity

5.1. College students undertake to follow the principle of academic integrity in carrying out scientific activities. In particular, violation of the principle of academic integrity in scientific activity occurs in the following cases:

5.1.1 Forgery, falsification or manipulation (for example, creation of non-existent data, correction of existing data in order to improve or worsen results). Typical cases of forgery, falsification or manipulation include the following:

- forgery of grades, results of evaluation of answers to the task;

- falsification of data (addition, insertion, correction), that is, measurements and results of observations of surveys, questionnaires and other methods when performing research;

- intentional tampering or spoiling of another student's assessed work;

- denial or concealment of the intellectual and/or financial contribution of specific individuals to scientific activities.

5.1.2 Theft or intentional damage of empirical research data, computer programs, samples of empirical materials, manuscripts.

5.1.3 Collusion and deception in order to obtain a better learning outcome:

- performing any graded work for another student

- cheating on the work of other students being evaluated;

- re-submission, delivery of already evaluated work;

- presentation of false excuses in case of non-fulfillment, late completion of the evaluated work;

- the performance of the assessed work by two or more students, which does not provide for group work;

- conscious help to other students: allowing him/her to copy off his/her assessed work, tips, using cheat sheets, textbooks, etc.

- presenting other people's evaluated works as your own.

5.1.4 Plagiarism, i.e. the appropriation of ideas, data or texts without specifying their authorship, i.e. the presentation of other people's ideas as their own. Plagiarism can manifest itself in various forms:

- quoting a source without using appropriate punctuation (quotation marks) and/or without specifying the source;

- rephrasing the source without specifying the source;

- using someone's ideas or arguments without reference to the author;

- submission of a written paper written in whole or in part by a friend or another student;

- presentation of a term paper/thesis taken from the Internet;

- presentation of a course paper that was completed as an assignment for another course.

- Academic bribery, i.e. giving remuneration for academic services (personal or group).

5.2. Violations of ethical standards of scientific activity are not considered, in particular, unconscious research errors, data conflict, as well as different interpretations of research results.


VI. Familiarization with the Code of Academic Ethics, compliance with its requirements and control over its implementation

6.1. This Code is published after its adoption and is widely accessible to members of the academic community. New students are familiarized with the requirements of the Code.

6.2. Each member of the academic community is obliged to comply with the requirements of this Code.


Responsibility and penalties

Participants in the educational process should remember that they are morally responsible for violating the principles of academic integrity. Plagiarism, the purchase and sale of term papers, theses is considered as going beyond a simple violation of the rules and is perceived as a serious violation against the community and entails punishment for using other people's materials. Teachers record each case of violation, and the student's current or final work is not allowed to be evaluated. The student is reprimanded, then up to expulsion. Teachers ensure that students are informed that students' work is checked for plagiarism using a web-based system.


VII. Final provisions

7.1. This Code comes into force the day after its approval by the College's Teaching Council and must be immediately published in such a way that all members of the College's academic community have access to it.
